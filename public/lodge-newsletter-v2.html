Lodge Advances to 67% on Path to Excellence Award

Harmony Lodge continues its steady march toward the prestigious Lion and Pillar Lodge of Excellence Award, having now completed fifteen of the twenty-two requirements needed for recognition at the Grand Lodge Annual Communication.

The past fortnight has seen significant progress. Brother David Mitchell organized and led a successful visitation to the VA Medical Center in Durham, where lodge members spent an afternoon with veterans in the rehabilitation ward. This effort secured completion of Patriotism Category Item 2.

Meanwhile, Brother James Caldwell returned from Wilkerson College with renewed enthusiasm for Masonic education, having spent a weekend immersed in the deeper teachings of the Craft. His attendance fulfills Education Category Item 3.

"Every brother who walks through our lodge door strengthens the bonds of our fraternity. We are but seven items from Excellence‚Äîand each of you holds the key."

‚Äî Worshipful Master Robert Harrison

The Working Tools Illuminated

At our January 7th Communication, Brother Thomas Richardson delivered a masterful presentation on "The Working Tools of a Fellowcraft." His exploration of the plumb, square, and level drew connections between the operative tools of ancient stonemasons and the speculative lessons they teach modern Masons about moral uprightness, fair dealing, and equality before the Great Architect.

The ensuing discussion proved lively, with several brothers sharing personal reflections on applying these principles in their professional lives. Such exchanges remind us why attendance matters‚Äîthe education continues well after the lecture concludes.

Charitable Works Continue Strong

Our partnership with the Community Food Pantry has yielded remarkable results this quarter: forty-seven volunteer hours contributed, one hundred fifty-six families served, and $2,340 raised for operational support. The February 15th Pancake Breakfast will benefit the Masonic Home for Children at Oxford.
